The Value of Linked Data

A musical artist is more than a collection of text, pictures and binary audio or video streams online. Central to his identity is his story, his artistic techniques, his musical style and evolution over time, pieces of context that gets lost amid masses of scattered information online.


Linked data strings these pieces of context together into the unique identity of any given artist:

  • When we search for all singles from Ferry Corsten, it automatically returns the song “Out of the Blue” by System F, Corsten’s alias in the 1990’s.

  • It understands “that song by Luther Vandross” could mean it’s written by him, performed by him, or both.

  • Linked data delivers the context behind the artist, granular enough that each album or single could have its individual stories, yet structured enough to paint a holistic picture of an artist, or genre through time.

At LinkedInsight, we utilized linked data to design a highly usable and interactive timeline curated to your musical interests.

Linked Data Triples

A RDF triple is a (1)subject-(2)predicate-(3)object expression, which is very similar to what it means in English:

Subject:
English - the thing we’re talking about
RDF - this is the resource

Predicate:
English - a term to describe or modify the subject
RDF - used to denote relationships between the subject and the object

Object:
English - the object is the thing that the verb, or predicate, is acting upon
RDF - the “target” or “value” of the triple, which can be another resource, or just a literal value such as a number or word
